        - Supply schedule and supply curve
        - Individual vs. market supply

            - Tradable permits
            - Subsidies
            - Government direct provision
            - Contracting out

            - Legislations and regulations

        - Common-pool resources as a type of externality
        - Common-pool resources and sustainability

            - Community solutions: collective governance
            - International cooperation
    2.9 Market failure: public goods
        - Defining characteristics of public goods
        - Allocative inefficiency associated with public goods
        - Solutions to public goods: government direct provision vs. contracting out

        - Adverse selection
        - Morale hazard

        - Perfect competition and allocative efficiency

        - Monopolistic competition: efficiency vs variety tradeoffs
            - Assumptions of monopolistic competition
            - Pros and cons of monopolistic competiton
        - Oligopoly: price stability
            - Assumptions of oligopoly
            - Measures of market concentration: concentration ratio
            - Collusive vs. non-collusive oligopolies
            - Price vs. non-price competitions
            - Game-theoretic approach to understanding oligopolies
            - Pros and cons of oligopolies
        - Monopoly
            - Assumptions of monopoly
            - Monopoly and allocative inefficiency
            - Monopoly and dynamic efficiency
            - Pros and cons of a monopoly
    2.12 Market's inability to achieve equity
        - Equity, equality, inequity, and inequality
        - Reasons why equity may not be achieved
        - Equity vs. efficiency
        - Redistribution of income as a solution

        - Measurement of poverty
        - Consequences of poverty and poverty cycle

        - Market-based supply-side policies
            - Pro-competition policies
            - Labor market reforms
            - Incentives-related policies
        - Evaluation of supply-side policies: time lags, demand-side implications, impacts on unemployment and equity, etc.
