**Original Article:** [Rising Trade Tensions Threaten Global Supply Chains](https://www.ft.com/content/example-trade-tensions)

## News Summary

Recent geopolitical tensions have led to increased trade barriers between major economies, threatening to disrupt global supply chains that were already strained from previous years' disruptions. The article examines how new tariffs, export controls, and "friend-shoring" policies are reshaping international trade patterns. Several multinational corporations are reconsidering their global manufacturing footprints, with some moving production closer to home markets or to politically aligned countries. The analysis suggests these trends could lead to higher consumer prices, reduced efficiency, and potential inflationary pressures. Economists debate whether these developments represent a temporary adjustment or a more permanent shift away from the hyper-globalization of recent decades.

## IB Economics Analysis

This article provides rich material for analyzing concepts from **Unit 4: The Global Economy** and **Unit 2: Microeconomics**.

### Relevant Theories and Frameworks:

1. **International Trade Benefits (Unit 4.1):** The article illustrates the potential costs of moving away from free trade, connecting to the theory of comparative advantage. Students can analyze how trade restrictions might reduce global efficiency and welfare.

2. **Trade Protection (Unit 4.2):** The discussion of tariffs, export controls, and other barriers provides concrete examples of trade protection measures. Students can analyze:
   - The economic effects of tariffs using supply and demand diagrams
   - The difference between tariffs and non-tariff barriers
   - The impact on consumer and producer surplus

3. **Free Trade vs. Trade Protection Debate (Unit 4.2):** The article presents arguments from both sides of this ongoing debate, including:
   - Efficiency gains from specialization vs. strategic autonomy concerns
   - Consumer benefits from lower prices vs. producer protection
   - Short-term adjustment costs vs. long-term strategic benefits

4. **Supply and Demand Analysis (Unit 2):** Students can use microeconomic tools to analyze how trade restrictions affect:
   - Prices of imported goods
   - Domestic production levels
   - Consumer choice and welfare

5. **Market Failure (Unit 2.5):** The article touches on potential market failures in global trade, such as:
   - Negative externalities from long supply chains (environmental costs)
   - Strategic considerations that might lead to suboptimal outcomes

### Evaluation Points for IB Assessments:

1. **Costs and Benefits of Globalization:** Students could evaluate whether the benefits of hyper-globalization outweigh the risks exposed by recent disruptions.

2. **Effectiveness of Trade Policies:** An evaluation could assess whether trade protection measures effectively achieve their stated goals (economic security, job protection) or create unintended consequences.

3. **Distributional Effects:** The article provides material for evaluating how trade policy changes affect different stakeholders (consumers, workers, shareholders, governments).

4. **Long-term vs. Short-term Considerations:** Students could evaluate the trade-offs between short-term adjustment costs and long-term strategic benefits of supply chain restructuring.

This article could support an IA exploring:
- The economic impact of specific trade barriers on a particular industry
- Comparative analysis of trade policies in different countries
- The relationship between trade policy and inflation
