**Original Article:** [US likely to raise temporary global tariff rate to 15% this week, Bessent says](https://www.reuters.com/world/china/new-global-us-15-tariff-rate-expected-kick-this-week-treasury-secretary-bessent-2026-03-04/)

## News Summary

U.S. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent announced that President Donald Trump's temporary global import tariff is expected to increase from 10% to 15% sometime during the week of March 4, 2026. This tariff adjustment follows a Supreme Court decision that struck down Trump's previous global tariffs imposed under national emergency powers. The new tariffs are being implemented under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974, which allows for temporary tariffs lasting up to 150 days unless extended by Congress. Bessent explained that during this 150-day period, studies from the U.S. Trade Representative on Section 301 tariffs and from the Commerce Department on Section 232 national security tariffs will be completed. He expressed confidence that these alternative tariff authorities would enable the U.S. to restore tariff rates to their previous levels within five months, describing these mechanisms as "slow moving, but more robust" than the emergency powers previously used. The policy shift represents a significant development in U.S. trade relations and could have substantial impacts on global trade flows, import prices, and international economic diplomacy.
