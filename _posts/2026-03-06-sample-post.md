**Original Article:** [Central Banks Grapple with Persistent Inflation](https://www.economist.com/finance-and-economics/2026/03/06/central-banks-grapple-with-persistent-inflation)

## News Summary

Major central banks worldwide continue to face challenges with persistent inflation rates that remain above target levels despite aggressive monetary tightening over the past several years. The Federal Reserve, European Central Bank, and Bank of England are all signaling a cautious approach to interest rate cuts, with policymakers expressing concerns about sticky services inflation and wage growth. Recent data shows headline inflation in developed economies averaging 3.2%, still above the typical 2% target, with core inflation (excluding volatile food and energy prices) proving particularly stubborn. The article examines the structural factors contributing to this persistence, including demographic shifts, deglobalization trends, and climate-related supply constraints. Central bankers are balancing the risks of premature easing against the economic costs of maintaining restrictive policies for extended periods.

## IB Economics Analysis

This news article provides excellent material for analyzing several key concepts from the IB Economics syllabus, particularly from **Unit 3: Macroeconomics** and **Unit 4: The Global Economy**.

### Relevant Theories and Frameworks:

1. **Macroeconomic Objectives (Unit 3.3):** The central banks' struggle with inflation directly relates to the macroeconomic objective of maintaining "low and stable rate of inflation." The article illustrates the challenges policymakers face in achieving this objective while balancing other goals like economic growth.

2. **Monetary Policy (Unit 3.4):** The discussion of interest rate decisions provides a real-world application of monetary policy tools. Students can analyze:
   - How central banks use interest rates to influence aggregate demand
   - The transmission mechanism of monetary policy
   - The trade-offs between inflation control and economic growth

3. **Phillips Curve (Unit 3.3):** The persistence of inflation despite economic slowing suggests a potential shift in the Phillips Curve relationship between inflation and unemployment. This connects to the "trade-offs between objectives" concept.

4. **AD-AS Model (Unit 3.2):** The article's discussion of supply-side constraints (demographic shifts, deglobalization, climate issues) provides material for analyzing both demand-pull and cost-push inflation using the AD-AS framework.

5. **Global Economic Interdependence (Unit 4):** The coordinated yet divergent approaches of different central banks illustrate the challenges of macroeconomic policy in an interconnected global economy.

### Evaluation Points for IB Assessments:

1. **Effectiveness of Monetary Policy:** Students could evaluate whether traditional monetary policy tools remain effective in addressing current inflation dynamics, considering structural changes in the global economy.

2. **Policy Trade-offs:** The article highlights the difficult trade-offs central banks face - between controlling inflation and supporting economic growth, and between different policy timeframes.

3. **Distributional Effects:** An evaluation could consider how persistent inflation and the policy response affect different groups in society (creditors vs. debtors, fixed-income earners, etc.).

4. **Uncertainty in Economic Forecasting:** The cautious approach of central banks reflects the inherent uncertainty in economic forecasting - a key concept in IB Economics.
