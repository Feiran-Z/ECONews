**Original Article:** [Ticketmaster parent Live Nation settles DOJ antitrust case](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/live-nation-settles-us-antitrust-case-2026-03-09/)

## News Summary

Live Nation Entertainment, the parent company of Ticketmaster, has reached a settlement with the U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ) to resolve antitrust allegations that the company monopolized live event markets. The settlement requires Live Nation to pay $280 million and make significant changes to its business practices. According to the DOJ, Live Nation used its dominant position in ticket sales and concert promotion to stifle competition, resulting in higher prices and fewer choices for consumers. The company will now be required to open its Ticketmaster platform to other ticket sellers and refrain from retaliating against venues that choose to work with competitors. This settlement concludes a high-profile antitrust case that began after public outcry over ticket sales for Taylor Swift's Eras Tour in 2022, which highlighted concerns about market concentration in the live events industry. The DOJ argued that Live Nation's practices violated antitrust laws by maintaining a monopoly through exclusionary contracts and anti-competitive behavior.

## IB Economics Connections

This case provides a real-world example of market failure due to market power, specifically monopoly power, which is covered in IB Economics Unit 2.11: Market failure: market power. Live Nation's dominance in live event ticketing and promotion illustrates how monopolies can lead to allocative inefficiency, where price exceeds marginal cost (P > MC), resulting in deadweight loss to society. The case demonstrates the characteristics of monopoly markets: high barriers to entry, significant market share, and the ability to influence prices. From an IB Economics perspective, students can analyze this case using several frameworks. First, they can evaluate the welfare effects using consumer and producer surplus diagrams to show how monopoly power reduces total social surplus. Second, they can examine the government's role in addressing market failure through antitrust regulation, a form of government intervention discussed in Unit 2.7. The settlement represents a regulatory approach to promoting competition, which aligns with supply-side policies aimed at improving market efficiency (Unit 3.7). Students could also consider the trade-offs between dynamic efficiency (innovation potential of large firms) and static efficiency (allocative and productive efficiency), evaluating whether breaking up monopolies might reduce incentives for innovation while increasing competition.
