**Original Article:** [Fed faced with hard choice on weak jobs, high inflation](https://www.reuters.com/business/fed-rate-cut-bets-rise-after-weak-jobs-data-2026-03-06/)

## News Summary

The U.S. Federal Reserve faces a difficult policy dilemma as weakening employment data and rising oil prices create conflicting pressures on monetary policy. In February 2026, employers unexpectedly shed jobs, pushing the unemployment rate up to 4.4%, while oil prices surged to $90 per barrel following geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, causing U.S. gasoline prices to jump from $3 to $3.32 per gallon within a week. This combination of labor market weakness and inflationary pressures from energy costs places the Fed in what policymakers describe as a "stagflation vise" - simultaneously confronting risks to both employment and price stability. Federal Reserve officials are divided on the appropriate response, with some emphasizing the need to support the labor market through potential rate cuts, while others advocate maintaining current restrictive policy to combat persistently elevated inflation, which remains at 2.9% - well above the Fed's 2% target. The central bank is expected to hold interest rates steady at its March meeting while traders increasingly bet on rate cuts beginning in June.
