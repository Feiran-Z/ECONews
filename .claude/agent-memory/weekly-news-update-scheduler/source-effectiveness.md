## Source Quality Assessment Criteria

### Reliability Score (1-5)
1. **Accuracy**: Factual correctness and data verification
2. **Timeliness**: Current relevance of information
3. **Depth**: Analytical depth vs. superficial coverage
4. **Objectivity**: Balanced presentation of different perspectives
5. **Educational value**: Suitability for IB Economics curriculum

## Optimization Recommendations

### Source Rotation Strategy
1. **Primary sources**: Financial Times, The Economist for comprehensive coverage
2. **Secondary sources**: Reuters, Bloomberg for timely data
3. **Specialized sources**: Academic journals for theoretical depth
4. **Official sources**: Government/agency reports for policy details

### Geographic Balance
- Ensure at least 2 geographic regions represented per topic
- Prioritize global perspectives for international topics
- Include developing country viewpoints for development economics

### Quality Control
- Minimum reliability score of 3 for any source used
- Multiple source verification for controversial topics
- Regular review and update of source effectiveness ratings
