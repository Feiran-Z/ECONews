### High Educational Value Topics
1. **Real-world applications**: Topics with clear current examples
2. **Policy relevance**: Government decisions affecting economies
3. **Global connections**: International economic relationships
4. **Data-driven**: Topics with statistical evidence available
5. **Student relevance**: Issues affecting young people

### Lower Educational Value (Need Creative Approaches)

3. **Methodological topics**: How economics is studied
4. **Technical details**: Specific calculation methods

## Coverage Strategy by Unit

### Unit 1: Introduction to Economics
- **Challenge**: Abstract concepts, low news frequency
- **Strategy**: Connect to current economic debates
- **Examples**: Scarcity in pandemic, opportunity cost of policies
- **Timing**: Early coverage to establish foundations

### Unit 2: Microeconomics
- **Strength**: Many real-world examples
- **Strategy**: Use business news, consumer behavior, policy cases
- **Examples**: Company monopolies, environmental regulations, price controls
- **Timing**: Spread throughout schedule

### Unit 3: Macroeconomics
- **Strength**: Constant news coverage
- **Strategy**: Align with economic data releases, policy announcements
- **Examples**: Inflation reports, budget announcements, unemployment data
- **Timing**: Regular coverage, match economic calendar

### Unit 4: The Global Economy
- **Strength**: International relations focus
- **Strategy**: Use trade agreements, currency news, development reports
- **Examples**: Trade wars, exchange rate fluctuations, SDG progress
- **Timing**: Match international summits, trade negotiations
